4C Names 2011 'Champions for Children' 

January 28 gala will 'sing their praises,' present first youth award 

  

(Cincinnati--August 29, 2011) 4C for Children, the region's leading resource in early education and care, will honor the 2011 Champions for Children at its annual Champions for Children Gala. This year's event will be held at the Westin Cincinnati on January 28, 2012.

 

The 2011 Champions for Children to be honored: 

  • State Senator Eric Kearney  
  • Community volunteer Digi Schueler  
  • Toyota Motor Engineering and Manufacturing North American 

4C, looking forward to Cincinnati's hosting of the International Choir Games in July 2012, will celebrate this year's Champions with a gala evening to "sing their praises." A local choir will set the tone as visitors arrive for the 6:30 pm cocktail hour. A 7:30 program and dinner will be followed by dancing from 9 to 11 pm to the music of 2nd Wind. 

 

This is the sixth year 4C has named Champions for Children. "4C is in a unique position to turn the spotlight on those who work to ensure a bright future for the children of our community," says 4C President/CEO Sallie Westheimer. "By so doing, 4C hopes to inspire others to become involved in addressing our community's continuing need to ensure that all children have a positive experience and are prepared for success in school and life." 

 

This year's event will also include presentation of the first "Champions for Children: The Next Generation" award to a project involving high school students taking action on behalf of the children of our community--or of our world. 4C's goal in launching this award, explains Ms. Westheimer, is to "reinforce in the leaders of tomorrow the importance of investing in the children of today--especially those from birth to age 8." The deadline for nominations is Sept. 15. For more about this award and a nomination form 

 

Letters signed by Michael Fisher, president/CEO of Cincinnati Children's Hospital and Medical Center, and Edwin J. "Ed" Rigaud, president/CEO of EnovaPremier, Inc., have gone out to area businesses inviting event sponsorships. Local 12 is the first to sign on as a "Quartet Sponsor" and will provide video services and a Local 12 anchor as emcee for the event. For more about event sponsor opportunities  

 

Cochairs Marla Fuller and Victoria Ames are leading the 4C Champions for Children Gala Committee which includes: Mary Kay Irwin, Joyce Monger, Carole Rigaud, Keisha Steward, Mary Beth Sundermann, Patti Towbin, Joelle Tunning, Merri Gaither Smith and Ann Williams.

4C for Children, the pioneering leader, advocate and resource for early childhood education and care, serves 23 counties from offices in Cincinnati, Dayton (Ohio) and Newport (Kentucky). Each year this not-for-profit assists over 8,000 families of all income levels in finding and choosing quality child care; educates over 10,000 child care providers in its workshops and trainings; works to increase the supply of high-quality early education and care; and advocates for children and families at the local, state and federal levels. For more information, www.4cforchildren.org.
